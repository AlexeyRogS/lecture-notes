5.1 The Hydrogen Hamiltonian

We will now look at the major early success story of quantum mechanics, solving for the motion of an electron in a hydrogen atom. Our assumptions are that the nucleus (the proton) is a particle with mass mp and charge +e, and the electron is a particle with mass me and charge ‚àíe. Reality, or rather our latest understanding of the hydrogen atom, is more complex and there are things that we are neglecting here such as coupling between spin and angular momentum, special relativity, and the quantum vacuum.
